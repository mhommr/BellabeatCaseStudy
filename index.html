Prepare

*For this case study, Sršen has suggested that we use the ‘FitBit Fitness Tracker Data’ available through Kaggle.*

Data Integrity:

This dataset was posted by user Möbius, who is competitions master and is a trusted user. Kaggle has also rated this dataset a 10 for usability, and this information is all public domain.

‘FitBit Fitness Tracker Data’ is a dataset containing health information for 30 users over a 30 day period. It includes information about calories burned, sleep hours, weight loss, etc. While it provides great information for the subject we are looking for, it is a very small sample size. However according to the Central Limit Theorem, 30 as a minimum is acceptable to represent the larger population. Bellabeat is specifically marketed for women, and this data does not specify gender or age, nor is there any information about water intake. So it is a more general sample of health tech users.

Analyze

What Makes a Healthy/Happy User?

After some research, the statistics deemed ideal for adult women are:

  • Recommended to get between 7-9 hours of sleep a night
  • Recommended to get around 10,000 steps a day
  • Recommended to burn around 2,000 calories a day

To begin my analysis I began using SQL. I wanted to see how often these goals were beat. There were 940 unique days logged for the 30 users. Of those days, 61% (571 days) beat the calorie goal, and only 32% (303 days) bat the step goal. Important distinction, for the sleep data, there were only 24 users that submitted data, and not every night was logged. There are 413 unique nights of sleep data, of those 46% (191 days) beat the sleep goal. Based on the data that I have, I'm going to use R for visualizations to help round out my analysis. Click here is my R analysis.

Summary

I focused on sleep and how the data collected affected that. Of the 413 days that were logged, only 46% were a full recommended night's sleep. My original hypothesis was proven wrong, that active minutes had a correlation to sleep time. In fact, it was sedentary time that had a negative correlation with sleep. So the more time spent sedentary, the less sleep users got. Excluding normal sleeping hours, 5AM-8AM had the lowest amount of activity logged. Friday, Tuesday, and Thursday have the lowest average sleep hours, overlapping slighlty with the days with the highest average sedentary days, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday.

Act

In order to increase sleep time, Bellabeat should focus on decreasing sedementary times. To do so, Bellabeat should focus on suggesting walks for users. This would help increase days that meet daily step goal, which is only about 32% of the days. The best times to blast a notice are between 5AM-7AM (excluding normal sleeping hours). These are the hours with the highest logged sedementary time. Another time would be from 6-8PM (after work) which is the most popular time to excercise. Tuesday-Thursday are the days with the highest sedementary times, so midweek would be another good time to spread reminders to users to get moving.
